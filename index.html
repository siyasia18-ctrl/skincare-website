The Power of Acids in Skincare

In luxury skincare, acids are the unsung heroes — refining texture, brightening tone, and renewing the skin’s surface for a luminous complexion.

Salicylic Acid – The Clarifier

Derived from willow bark, salicylic acid is a beta hydroxy acid (BHA) that penetrates deep into pores to dissolve excess oil and impurities. It is exceptional for refining texture and minimizing breakouts without stripping the skin. Luxury formulations balance its potency with soothing ingredients like green tea or squalane, ensuring clarity with comfort.

Glycolic Acid – The Refiner

As the smallest alpha hydroxy acid (AHA), glycolic acid gently loosens dull surface cells to reveal new radiance beneath. Regular use enhances smoothness, fades discoloration and pigmentation, and improves absorption of serums and creams.

Lactic Acid – The Softener

Lactic acid offers exfoliation with elegance — hydrating while it refines. Sourced from milk or fermented plants, it boosts luminosity and suppleness, making it ideal for dry or sensitive skin types.

How to Use Acids Wisely

Rotate acids based on your skin’s needs, and avoid layering too many actives in one routine. Always follow with a rich moisturizer and SPF — as exfoliation makes skin more light-sensitive. The result: a complexion that’s polished, not punished.
